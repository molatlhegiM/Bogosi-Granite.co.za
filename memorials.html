Single Headstones

A single headstone is a vertical monument placed at the head of a grave to memorialize a single individual. It is typically made of two stones - an upright tablet and a rectangular block base.

A single upright headstone is the most common type of memorial for honoring the life and memory of a single person.

Double Headstones

A double headstone is a vertical stone memorial consisting of at least two connected stones - a vertical tablet and a long rectangular base. It is designed to honor and commemorate the lives of two or more family members who are buried side by side.

Previous Next

Grave Ledger

A grave ledger is a large stone slab that covers all or most of a grave. It is a prominent and substantial memorial, offering ample space for inscriptions, and other adornments. Full ledger grave markers vary in design, ranging from simple and flush with the ground to more elaborate structures with multiple tiers and intricate sculptures.

They can be used as standalone markers or placed alongside larger family memorials, providing a comprehensive and personalized tribute to the deceased.

Grave Marker

A grave marker is a simple limestone/granite stone with a stick at the back placed at a grave right after the burial of an individual who has passed away. Typically installed at the head of the grave, to mark the grave for the bereaved family.

Memorial Niche Plaque

A granite marker, usually placed at the crematorium section on the wall of remembrance at your local church or cemetery, mounted on an existing tombstone or to be placed in your garden at home

It serves as a personalized and lasting tribute, bearing the name, dates of birth and death, and symbolic adornment that reflects the individual's life and legacy.
